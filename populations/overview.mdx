A **population** is a reusable, validated set of synthetic agents grounded in census data, behavioral science, and your real customer data. Every simulation runs against one population. Better population → better simulation.

## What a population contains

```
Population
├── Agents (1,000 – 100,000)
│   ├── Demographic profile (census-grounded)
│   ├── Behavioral profile (adopter category, decision biases)
│   ├── Usage history (from your analytics)
│   └── Memory (verbatims retrieved from your qualitative data)
├── Segments (auto-tagged + custom cohorts)
├── Validation report (5-experiment behavioral suite)
└── Drift status (compared to baseline demographic mix)
```

## Where the grounding comes from

| Layer | Source | What it contributes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Demographic baseline** | US/EU Census Bureau | Age, income, education, region, household composition |
| **Behavioral priors** | Sentia's behavioral science library | Adopter categories, decision biases, social proof, loss aversion, urgency response |
| **Usage history** | Your analytics (Amplitude / Mixpanel / PostHog) | Feature adoption, retention, frequency, cohort membership |
| **Qualitative memory** | Your interviews + support (Dovetail / Intercom / Zendesk) | Real verbatims clustered into pain points, retrieved during simulation |

The first two are always present. The last two are what separate ~70% accuracy populations from ~85% accuracy populations.

## Why "reusable"

A population is built once and used many times. Cost is paid up-front (generation + validation, typically 5–15 minutes for 1,000 agents). Simulations against the population are then fast.

Most workspaces have 3–8 active populations:

- A broad "all customers" population for high-level roadmap questions
- 2–4 segment-specific populations (e.g., "SMB trial users", "Enterprise admins", "Power users")
- 1–2 special-purpose populations (e.g., "Churned users", "New signups")

You can have many. There's no limit beyond contract allocation.

## Population health

Each population has three health signals:

| Signal | Healthy threshold | What it means |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Validation pass rate** | ≥ 80% | Fraction of behavioral experiments where agents respond like real humans |
| **Fidelity score** | ≥ 0.7 | Overall behavioral realism — composite of pass rate, verbatim retrieval quality, and demographic coverage |
| **Drift** | < 10% | How much the population's demographic mix has diverged from baseline since build |

The Populations list view shows all three at a glance. Rebuild any population with drift > 10% — it takes < 10 minutes and keeps accuracy up.

## Lifecycle

```
DRAFT          ──→ BUILDING ──→ VALIDATING ──→ READY ──→ DRIFTED ──→ ARCHIVED
(configuring)      (~5-15min)    (~2min)       (use it)  (rebuild)   (history only)
```

You can pause a building population at any time. Validation is automatic on first build; you can re-run it anytime.

Archived populations stay in your workspace history for audit purposes but can't be used in new simulations.
