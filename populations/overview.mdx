A **population** is a reusable cohort of synthetic users. Every simulation runs against one population, so cohort fit is the strongest determinant of whether a report reads like useful research or generic feedback.

In the current app, populations are built as **10-100 agent behavioral cohorts**. Use multiple focused cohorts rather than one broad "all users" population.

## What a population contains

```text
Population
├── Agents (10-100 in the UI path)
│   ├── Demographic profile
│   ├── Behavioral patterns
│   ├── Psychological drivers
│   ├── Research memories from attached artifacts
│   └── Optional custom trait scores
├── Cohort description and target audience
├── Health and qualitative coverage signals
└── Simulation history
```

## Where grounding comes from

| Layer | Source | What it contributes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Audience brief** | Your natural-language description | The main steering input for who agents should represent. |
| **Demographics** | ACS PUMS for US cohorts; WorldPop and World Bank for global cohorts | Age, gender, education, occupation, location, and region-specific context. |
| **Behavior patterns** | Population Composer selections and inference | Workarounds, setup abandonment, comparison shopping, support escalation, and similar patterns. |
| **Psychological drivers** | Trait presets and optional custom traits | Risk aversion, novelty seeking, trust seeking, privacy sensitivity, automation skepticism, and more. |
| **Research artifacts** | Resource Library uploads | Interview language, survey responses, persona context, recordings, and prior experiment evidence. |

## What makes a good cohort

Good cohorts are:

- **Specific** enough that agents share a meaningful context.
- **Research-backed** enough that voices do not feel generic.
- **Reusable** across several related studies.
- **Small enough** to understand and iterate quickly.

Examples:

- "First-time enterprise admins connecting Figma during onboarding."
- "Procurement-sensitive design leaders evaluating pricing-page trust."
- "Accessibility-reliant users completing a setup flow."
- "Power users comparing workflow shortcuts."

## Population lifecycle

```text
Draft -> Building -> Ready -> Used in simulations -> Refined or duplicated
```

You can rename, duplicate, archive, and delete populations. Deletion can be blocked while a population is building or when simulations depend on it.

## What to review before using a population

Open the population detail and check:

- Agent count and status.
- Sample personas.
- Demographic panels.
- Behavior trait radar.
- Adoption or readiness profile.
- Qualitative coverage.
- Validation or health signals when available.
- Simulation history.

If a population looks wrong, fix it before interpreting simulation results.
