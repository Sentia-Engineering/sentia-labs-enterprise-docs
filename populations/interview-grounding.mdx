title: "Interview grounding"
description: "How real customer verbatims become agent memory — and why this is the single biggest accuracy lever in Sentia."
---

Populations grounded only in demographic data top out around **~70% normalized accuracy**. Populations grounded in real customer interviews, support conversations, and verbatims hit **~85%** (Park et al. 2024, validated on Sentia workspaces).

This page explains how grounding works and how to maximize its impact.

## The pipeline

```
Qualitative source         Pinecone vector store        Agent memory
(Dovetail, Intercom,  ──→  (Cohere embeddings,    ──→  (retrieved at run time
 Zendesk)                   clustered themes)            via similarity scoring)
```

Three stages:

1. **Ingest** — pull conversations, transcripts, and tickets from connected qualitative sources
2. **Embed** — chunk verbatims, embed via Cohere, store in Pinecone with persona + segment metadata
3. **Retrieve** — at simulation time, each agent's profile is used as a query; top-K relevant verbatims (scored by `recency × importance × relevance`) become part of that agent's Memory step

## Sources

### Dovetail — preferred

- **What you get:** structured interview transcripts and highlights, organized by project
- **Best for:** workspaces with mature qualitative practice — scheduled interviews, tagged themes, highlight reels
- **Sync:** OAuth via Nango. Pulls transcripts hourly, highlights daily.
- **Volume sweet spot:** 50+ interviews across diverse personas

### Intercom

- **What you get:** support conversations + satisfaction ratings
- **Best for:** workspaces where most user feedback comes through support
- **Sync:** OAuth via Nango, every 24 hours
- **Filter:** by satisfaction rating, conversation tags, custom attributes

### Zendesk

- **What you get:** tickets, conversation history, satisfaction scores
- **Best for:** enterprise-y stacks where Zendesk is the canonical voice-of-customer surface
- **Sync:** OAuth via Nango, every 24 hours
- **Filter:** by ticket type, priority, satisfaction, custom fields

### Combining sources

You can ground a single population from multiple sources. Sentia de-duplicates verbatims across sources by content similarity.

A common high-leverage combination: **Dovetail (interviews) + Intercom (in-the-flow support feedback)**. Interviews give structured depth; support gives high-volume current sentiment.

## What "grounding" looks like in practice

Without grounding, an agent's Memory step looks generic:

> *"I value my time and am cautious about new tools. As an early adopter, I'd evaluate this change against my current workflow."*

With grounding (same agent), Memory pulls relevant real verbatims:

> *"From recent interviews: 'We tried shortening trials at our last company — the rushed feeling pushed power users away.' From support: 'The 14-day trial was perfect, I needed every day of it to convince my team.' From a Dovetail highlight: 'Trial length is a leading indicator of commitment quality.'"*

The Reflection and Decision steps that follow are dramatically more grounded.

## Scoring: recency × importance × relevance

When an agent retrieves memories, Sentia ranks verbatims by:

| Factor | What it captures |
| --- | --- |
| **Recency** | How recent the verbatim is. Decays exponentially. 30-day half-life by default. |
| **Importance** | Highlighted/tagged content + high-satisfaction-impact conversations score higher |
| **Relevance** | Cosine similarity between the agent's profile (query embedding) and the verbatim embedding |

Top 5–8 verbatims per agent end up in the Memory step. You can preview this in the [Population Builder](/populations/build) before generation.

## Volume guidelines

| Verbatim corpus size | Accuracy improvement vs. demographic-only |
| --- | --- |
| 0 (demographic only) | Baseline (~70%) |
| 1–50 verbatims | Small lift (~73%) |
| 100–500 | Meaningful (~78%) |
| 1,000–5,000 | Strong (~82%) |
| 5,000+ | Best in class (~85%+) |

Quality and persona diversity matter as much as volume. 200 diverse, well-tagged Dovetail interviews beat 5,000 unsorted support tickets.

## What to do if your corpus is small

For workspaces new to qualitative research:

1. **Connect Intercom or Zendesk** — usually the fastest path to 500+ verbatims
2. **Run a 10-interview discovery sprint** — concentrate on key segments and load into Dovetail
3. **Use NPS responses** — many workspaces have hundreds of NPS comments sitting unused
4. **Start small** — even 50 well-tagged verbatims beat zero. You'll see the lift immediately.

Your CSM can help you scope a "qualitative ramp" plan for the first 30 days.

## Privacy & compliance

- **All verbatims stay in your workspace's Pinecone namespace** — strict tenant isolation, no cross-workspace retrieval
- **PII redaction** — Sentia automatically detects and redacts emails, phone numbers, and full names in stored verbatims (configurable)
- **Right to erasure** — when a user requests deletion, Sentia cascades through PG, MongoDB, S3, *and* Pinecone — verbatims are permanently removed
- **EU residency** — workspaces on EU residency use eu-west-1 Pinecone region

See [Security & data handling](/security/data-handling).

## What grounding can't do

- Agents won't predict reactions to products totally outside their experience (e.g., a B2C agent population on an enterprise SaaS feature)
- Verbatims about old features may anchor agents on outdated framings — refresh the corpus periodically
- Grounding doesn't fix bad input quality — show agents real UI for full accuracy
