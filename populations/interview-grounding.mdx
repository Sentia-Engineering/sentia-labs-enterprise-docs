Research grounding is how Sentia moves beyond generic synthetic users. Uploaded artifacts give agents language, concerns, motivations, and context that resemble the people your team studies.

## The pipeline

```text
Research artifacts -> parsing and summarization -> memory signals -> cohort agents
```

Artifacts can come from the [Resource Library](/resource-library/research-artifacts) or from inline attachments while building a population.

## Good grounding sources

| Source | Why it helps |
| --- | --- |
| **Interview transcripts** | Deep motivations, vocabulary, objections, and task context. |
| **Survey CSVs** | Patterned responses, NPS comments, and structured feedback. |
| **Persona documents** | Existing team knowledge about audience segments. |
| **Audio/video recordings** | Usability sessions, calls, and research conversations after transcription. |
| **A/B or conversion history** | Prior evidence that can inform calibration and expectation setting. |

## What grounded memory changes

Without grounding, an agent may say:

> "I want the setup to be clear and efficient."

With relevant research attached, the same type of agent can cite more specific context:

> "I need to know exactly what Figma access is reading before I connect it, because our design files include roadmap-sensitive work."

That specificity is what makes a simulation useful in critique.

## How much research is enough?

Start with quality, not volume:

| Corpus | Good use |
| --- | --- |
| **3-5 strong artifacts** | First run or focused concept test. |
| **10-20 artifacts** | Healthy first-week grounding for a cohort. |
| **50+ artifacts** | Stronger recurring cohort or high-stakes study. |

A small set of well-labeled, relevant interviews is usually better than a large dump of mixed, stale files.

## Grounding hygiene

- Attach artifacts that match the cohort, not the whole company archive.
- Prefer recent research when product context has changed.
- Include old research only when old expectations still matter.
- Rename files with audience, date, and topic.
- Re-parse files when metadata or classification was wrong.

## Signs grounding is weak

- Voices sound like generic product advice.
- Agents miss known objections from your research.
- The report focuses on irrelevant concerns.
- Different cohorts sound indistinguishable.

Fix weak grounding by narrowing the audience brief, attaching better artifacts, or creating a more specific custom trait.
