title: "Simulation lifecycle"

---

A simulation moves through a small set of stages. Knowing them helps you catch setup problems while a run is live and understand what appears in the completed report.

## Stages

<Steps>
  <Step title="Queued">
    The run is accepted, configuration is validated, and work is assigned.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Running">
    Agents evaluate the scenario in waves. The live page updates completion count, preference split, concerns, verbatims, and other metrics.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Converging">
    Sentia checks whether additional agents are still changing the answer. Divisive studies may stay less stable because audience slices genuinely disagree.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Completed">
    Final aggregation creates findings, concerns, recommendations, population analysis, voices, and methodology.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Failed or canceled">
    The run stops before completion. Inspect the failure reason, fix the setup if needed, and rerun.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What to monitor live

| Live signal | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| **Agents completed** | Confirms the run is moving. |
| **Preference split** | Shows early choice distribution for A/B and competitive studies. |
| **Concerns per agent** | Indicates friction density. |
| **Live verbatims** | Helps confirm agents understand the prompt and audience. |
| **Convergence** | Shows whether the distribution is stabilizing. |

## Confidence vs. convergence

These are different:

| Metric | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Convergence** | Whether adding agents is still changing the distribution. |
| **Confidence** | How strongly supported the result appears, given the responses and cohort context. |

A study can converge to a split result. That is not a failure; it often means the design works for one slice of the cohort and not another.
