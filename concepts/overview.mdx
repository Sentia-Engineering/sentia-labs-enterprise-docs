Sentia is organized around five concepts: **Design Agent**, **Simulations**, **Populations**, **Resource Library**, and the **Decision Lifecycle**.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Design Agent" icon="pen-ruler" href="/design-agent/overview">
    Import Figma files, generate editable surfaces, create variants, and hand selected designs to simulation.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Simulation" icon="flask" href="/simulations/overview">
    A synthetic user study against one scenario and one population.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Population" icon="users" href="/populations/overview">
    A reusable 10-100 agent cohort grounded in an audience brief, demographics, behavior traits, and research artifacts.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Resource Library" icon="file-lines" href="/resource-library/overview">
    Research uploads and saved simulation or comparison reports.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Decision Lifecycle" icon="rotate" href="/decisions/lifecycle">
    The longer loop for teams that connect simulations to shipping, calibration, and release evidence.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How they relate

```text
Research artifacts  ->  Population
Figma / Design Agent -> Simulation -> Report -> Resource Library
Report evidence      -> Design critique, product decision, or follow-up study
```

## Walking through a real flow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload context">
    A researcher adds onboarding interview transcripts and survey comments to the Resource Library.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build a cohort">
    The team creates "first-time enterprise admins connecting Figma" as a 50-agent population.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Prepare surfaces">
    A designer imports the onboarding Figma file into Design Agent and creates a security-forward variant.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run a simulation">
    The team sends both surfaces to simulation with the same prompt and population.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the report">
    The report shows that the security-forward variant reduces access concerns but adds setup friction.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save evidence">
    The team saves the comparison report to the Resource Library and uses it in critique.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What grounds the agents

Agents are not generic personas. They draw from:

| Source | What it contributes |
| --- | --- |
| **Audience brief** | Who the cohort should represent and what context matters. |
| **Demographic grounding** | Country, region, age, education, occupation, and related context. |
| **Behavioral library** | Patterns such as setup abandonment, price checking, trust seeking, and privacy sensitivity. |
| **Research artifacts** | Real language and concerns from transcripts, surveys, recordings, and persona docs. |
| **Custom traits** | Workspace-specific dimensions such as AI literacy or security sensitivity. |
