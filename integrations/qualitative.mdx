Qualitative integrations are optional enterprise connectors. In the current Figma-first path, many teams upload transcripts, surveys, and recordings directly through the [Resource Library](/resource-library/research-artifacts) before setting up automatic connectors.

Use these connectors when your team wants ongoing ingestion from a source system rather than manual uploads.

## Dovetail (preferred for interview-heavy teams)

Dovetail provides structured interview transcripts, highlights, and tagged themes — the highest-quality grounding source.

<Steps>
  <Step title="OAuth via Nango">
    Integrations → Dovetail → **Connect**. You'll be redirected to Dovetail to grant Sentia read access to projects, transcripts, and highlights.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose projects">
    Pick which Dovetail projects to ingest. Most teams start with all active projects from the last 12 months.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Initial sync">
    Sentia pulls transcripts and highlights. ~5–20 minutes for typical workspaces.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify grounding">
    Open Populations → existing population → **Sources**. You should see Dovetail listed with verbatim count and last-sync time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**What's synced:**
- Interview transcripts (full text)
- Highlights (clip-level)
- Tags and themes
- Project + participant metadata (anonymized at boundary)

**Sync cadence:** transcripts hourly, highlights daily.

**Best practice:** tag your highlights generously in Dovetail — Sentia uses tags as relevance signals during agent memory retrieval.

## Intercom

Best for workspaces with high-volume support conversations.

<Steps>
  <Step title="OAuth via Nango">
    Integrations → Intercom → **Connect**. Grant Sentia read access.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure filters">
    Pick:
    - **Conversation types:** New conversations, replies, or both
    - **Status filter:** Open, closed, snoozed
    - **Tags filter:** Only conversations with specific tags (optional)
    - **Satisfaction filter:** Only rated conversations (optional, recommended)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Initial sync">
    Pulls conversations from the last 90 days. Typical duration: 15–45 minutes depending on volume.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set ongoing sync window">
    Default: rolling 90-day window, refreshed every 24 hours.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**What's synced:** conversation content, customer satisfaction scores, conversation outcomes, customer attributes (anonymized).

**Best practice:** filter to rated conversations only — they have higher signal density than the average noisy support ticket.

## Zendesk

Same role as Intercom; pick whichever your team actually uses.

<Steps>
  <Step title="OAuth via Nango">
    Integrations → Zendesk → **Connect**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose ticket scope">
    - **Brands:** which Zendesk brands to ingest
    - **Ticket types:** standard, problem, incident, question, task
    - **Priority filter:** all, or high/urgent only
    - **Satisfaction filter:** rated only (recommended)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Initial sync">
    Last 90 days. 15–45 minutes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure ongoing sync">
    24-hour cadence.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Combining qualitative sources

You can connect multiple. Sentia de-duplicates by content similarity (cosine threshold) — if the same conversation exists in both Intercom and Zendesk (unusual but possible), it's counted once.

Common combinations:

- **Dovetail + Intercom** — structured interview depth + high-volume in-the-flow feedback
- **Dovetail + Zendesk** — same, with Zendesk as the in-the-flow source
- **Intercom only** — when interviews are sparse but support volume is high
- **Dovetail only** — when interviews are the primary research method

## PII redaction

Sentia auto-redacts PII before storing verbatims:

- Email addresses → `[EMAIL]`
- Phone numbers → `[PHONE]`
- Full names (when explicitly tagged in source) → `[NAME]`
- Credit card patterns → `[REDACTED]`

You can configure additional regex patterns in Settings → Integrations → Privacy.

## Sync health

Each qualitative integration shows:

- Total verbatims synced
- Net new in last 24 hours
- Last successful sync timestamp
- Active filter set

When a sync fails repeatedly (3 consecutive failures), the integration goes to **Error** state and admins get a Slack notification.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Verbatims aren't appearing in populations after sync">
    Verbatims attach to populations at build time, not at sync time. Existing populations don't auto-receive new verbatims — rebuild or refresh the population to pull in new grounding.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Sync is much slower than expected">
    Likely a provider rate limit. Intercom and Zendesk have per-account API limits Sentia respects. For very high-volume workspaces, sync may take overnight for first run; ongoing syncs are incremental and fast.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Agents are quoting outdated phrases ('our old design', etc.)">
    Your corpus is too historical. Shorten the rolling window (Integrations → integration card → **Sync window**) to 60 or 30 days, and refresh affected populations.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
