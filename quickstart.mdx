This quickstart gets a researcher or designer from a new workspace to the first useful simulation. The shortest path is: connect Figma, define a small cohort, add a design or text scenario, and read the report.

<Note>
  **Prerequisites:** Your workspace owner has finished SSO setup and invited you. If you don't have an invite, ping your CSM or [hello@sentialabs.io](mailto:hello@sentialabs.io).
</Note>

## Step 1 · Sign in

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the dashboard">
    Visit [app.sentialabs.ai](https://app.sentialabs.ai). You'll be redirected to your corporate identity provider (Okta, Entra ID, Google Workspace, etc.).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Learn the nav">
    The main areas are Overview (`O`), Design Agent (`D`), Simulations (`S`), Populations (`P`), Resource Library (`R`), and Settings (`⌘,`). Press `?` in the app to see shortcuts.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Sentia is desktop-first. Use a laptop or external monitor for Design Agent, simulation reports, and comparison work.
</Tip>

## Step 2 · Connect Figma

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    Press `I` or go to **Settings -> Integrations**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the workspace Figma token">
    Connect Figma with a personal access token that has `current_user:read`, `file_content:read`, and `file_comments:write`. See [Design Agent sources](/design-agent/sources).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm access">
    Paste a Figma file URL in Design Agent or the simulation wizard. If Sentia can read frames, setup is complete.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 3 · Build a first cohort

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Populations">
    Press `P`, then create a new population.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Describe the audience">
    Use a concrete research brief, such as "First-time enterprise workspace admins evaluating design-tool onboarding." Keep the first cohort narrow.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick size">
    Start with **50 agents**. Cohorts currently support **10-100 agents**; 50 is enough for first-pass design research while staying fast.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Attach research if you have it">
    Upload or attach transcripts, survey CSVs, persona docs, or recordings from the [Resource Library](/resource-library/research-artifacts). You can also build the first cohort from the brief alone.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 4 · Choose the design input

Pick one of two paths:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Use Design Agent" icon="pen-ruler" href="/design-agent/overview">
    Best when you want to import a Figma file, generate variants, and send selected surfaces to simulation.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Use the simulation wizard" icon="wand-magic-sparkles" href="/simulations/wizard">
    Best when you already know the study question and want to simulate Figma frames or a text scenario directly.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

For the fastest first run, start the simulation wizard with `N`.

## Step 5 · Run the 3-step simulation wizard

<Steps>
  <Step title="Decision">
    Choose **A/B Test**, **Open-ended**, or **Competitive**. Add the research question, scenario context, and visual input. Figma and plain text are the main first-week sources.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Population">
    Select the cohort you just built. The wizard only lets you launch against ready populations with usable agents.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure">
    Use **Fast** mode for the first pass and 10-50 agents depending on cohort size. Use **Deep** when the question is high-stakes or you need stronger evidence.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 6 · Watch it run

You'll land on the live run page. Watch these signals:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Agents completed" icon="users" href="/simulations/results">
    Shows how many agents have finished the study.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Preference split" icon="chart-bar" href="/simulations/results">
    For A/B and competitive runs, watch which option is gaining support.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Concerns per agent" icon="message-exclamation" href="/simulations/results">
    A rising concern rate means the design needs closer reading.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Live verbatims" icon="message" href="/simulations/results">
    Read early reasoning to catch obvious setup or audience mismatch issues.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Step 7 · Read and save the report

Completed simulations open as a narrative report. For a design study, read in this order:

1. **Verdict and visual comparison** - confirm the evaluated surfaces and headline outcome.
2. **Findings** - understand the strongest patterns.
3. **Concern hotspots** - locate confusing, risky, or inaccessible parts of the design.
4. **Recommendations** - turn objections into design follow-ups.
5. **Population lens and voices** - check who reacted differently and pull representative quotes.

Export or save the PDF when the evidence matters. Saved reports appear in [Resource Library -> Reports](/resource-library/reports).

## You're done

That's your first simulation. From here, you have three productive next steps:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Build a sharper cohort" icon="users-gear" href="/populations/build">
    Add research artifacts, behavior patterns, traits, and demographic filters.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Use Design Agent" icon="pen-ruler" href="/design-agent/overview">
    Import Figma, create variants, and send selected surfaces to simulation.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Upload research" icon="upload" href="/resource-library/research-artifacts">
    Bring transcripts, surveys, and recordings into the workspace.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
