Custom traits let your team define audience dimensions that matter for your product but are not captured by default demographic or behavioral fields.

Examples:

- AI literacy.
- Security sensitivity.
- Cost scrutiny.
- Accessibility reliance.
- Design-system familiarity.
- Procurement influence.
- Automation skepticism.

## When to use custom traits

Use them when a trait is important enough to appear repeatedly in analysis and simulation comparisons.

Good custom traits:

- Are specific to your product or market.
- Have observable evidence in research artifacts or cohort definitions.
- Help explain why one audience slice reacts differently.

Avoid traits that are just another name for a population. If the whole audience is "enterprise admins," make that a cohort, not a custom trait.

## Scoring methods

| Method | Use when |
| --- | --- |
| **Keyword match** | Research artifacts contain clear phrases that indicate the trait. |
| **Segment membership** | The trait maps to known user or account segments. |
| **Manual** | Researchers need to curate the score based on qualitative knowledge. |

Custom traits are applied during population build and can appear in downstream trait-correlation analysis.

## Governance

- Name traits in language researchers and designers already use.
- Document what high and low scores mean.
- Review old traits quarterly so the model does not accumulate unused dimensions.
- Coordinate with your CSM for high-stakes trait definitions.
