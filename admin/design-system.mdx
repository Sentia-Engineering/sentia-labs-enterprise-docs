---
title: "Design system settings"
description: "Give Sentia your design and accessibility rules so generated surfaces match your standards."
---

Admins can add workspace-level design guidance in **Settings -> Design system**. Sentia uses this context when generating Design Agent surfaces and when reporting accessibility issues in simulation results.

## Design document

Paste or upload a `.md` or `.txt` document that describes how your team designs product surfaces.

Good content includes:

- Component usage rules.
- Voice and tone guidance.
- Layout and spacing conventions.
- Form, table, modal, and navigation standards.
- Accessibility commitments.
- Product-specific patterns that should not be changed casually.

The design document is capped at 50,000 characters. Keep it practical: concise rules beat a dumped design-system site.

## Accessibility rule PDFs

You can upload up to five PDF rule documents. Use these for accessibility checklists, legal requirements, procurement standards, or internal audit guidance.

Simulation reports with visual inputs can surface an **Accessibility Audit** layer when relevant. The audit uses these workspace rules as context.

## Governance

- Keep the design document owned by a design-system lead or admin.
- Update it when the design system materially changes.
- Remove outdated PDFs so audits do not mix old and new guidance.
- Treat generated surfaces as proposals; final design-system compliance remains a team review responsibility.
