---
title: "Social dynamics"
description: "Experimental setting for peer-influenced simulation waves."
---

Social dynamics is an experimental workspace setting for modeling peer influence. When enabled, later simulation waves can see sampled peer rationales from earlier waves.

Use it only when social proof is part of the research question, such as:

- Adoption that depends on peer confidence.
- Collaboration workflows.
- Community or marketplace behavior.
- Enterprise rollouts where admins influence each other.

## Settings

| Setting | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Enabled** | Turns peer-rationale exposure on for new runs. |
| **Peer rationale count** | Number of prior rationales shown to later-wave agents. |
| **Peer scope** | Whether peer examples come from the same cohort or broader workspace context. |

Runs created while social dynamics is enabled are flagged so analysts know peer influence was part of the method.

## Caution

Most first-week onboarding should leave this setting off. Standard independent-agent runs are easier to explain, compare, and trust. Enable social dynamics only when the study question explicitly needs peer effects.
