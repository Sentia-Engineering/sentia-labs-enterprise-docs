The quickstart taught you to run a simulation using the sample population. This walks through the first one against a real upcoming decision in your team's roadmap.

## Pick a decision worth simulating

Good first-simulation candidates:

- A **pricing change** — new tier, increase, or restructure
- An **onboarding redesign** — new flow, removed steps, or trial change
- A **plan-tier feature shift** — moving a feature between plans, or adding a paywall
- A **navigation or IA change** — moving major sections, renaming, consolidation
- A **deprecation** — removing a feature used by some segments

Bad first-simulation candidates:

- **Copy tweaks** — token cost without much signal
- **Bug fixes** — agents don't have opinions on parity changes
- **Internal tooling** — agents simulate your *users*, not your team

## Set up the simulation

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the wizard">
    Press `N`. The 4-step wizard opens.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 1 — Decision">
    Pick **From scratch** (your built-in templates are useful later, but for a first real run write your own).
    
    Fill in:
    - **Question:** *"Should we move feature X from the Pro plan to the Enterprise plan?"*
    - **Hypothesis:** *"≥ 60% of current Pro users won't be disrupted because they don't use X. The ~40% who do will see it as the trigger to upgrade."*
    - **Success criteria:** *"No drop in Pro retention. ≥ 5% of Pro users who use X upgrade in 30 days."*
    
    These get attached to the decision and used in agent reasoning.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 2 — Scenario">
    Add at least one **capture**. Best-to-worst input quality:
    
    1. **Captured localhost UI** — point the [`@sentia/capture` CLI](/simulations/inputs#localhost-capture) at your local prototype
    2. **Figma frame** — paste a Figma URL; Sentia pulls the frame as image input
    3. **Captured URL** — point at your staging or production page
    4. **Text description** — written-only, lowest fidelity (~15–20% accuracy hit)
    
    For high-stakes decisions, use real UI. For exploratory runs, text is fine.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 3 — Population">
    Select the population you built in [Build your first population](/onboarding/first-population). Sentia auto-suggests **segments to focus on** based on your decision text — you can accept the defaults or pick specific adopter categories.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 4 — Configure">
    For your first real run, pick **Deep mode** with **2,000 agents**. Cost ≈ 6,000 LLM calls; runs in ~10–15 minutes. Lower for fast iteration, higher only if you need fine segment slicing.
    
    Click **Launch** or `⌘⇧L`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Watch it run

Open the live run view. Useful things to do while it runs:

- **Tail transcripts** to see individual agents reasoning — this is the fastest way to build intuition about what the agents "know"
- **Watch convergence** — if it stays amber through 800+ agents, the scenario may be genuinely divisive
- **Pause anytime** — you can stop the run if early waves show the answer; partial results are kept

## Read results honestly

The 8-tab results view tempts everyone to anchor on the Overview tab. Discipline yourself to read in this order:

<Steps>
  <Step title="1. Segments">
    Always start here. The overall distribution hides everything important. Look for: does the answer change dramatically by adopter category, plan tier, or tenure?
  </Step>
  <Step title="2. Objections">
    Read 5–10 verbatim-style objection quotes. If they sound nothing like your actual users, your population is under-grounded — pause the analysis and fix the population first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="3. Confidence + Convergence">
    Headline confidence > 0.8 *and* convergence > 0.95 → trust the number. Either below → directional only.
  </Step>
  <Step title="4. Overview">
    Now read the headline. By this point you have enough context to weigh it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="5. Recommendations">
    Sentia-generated actions to address objections. Treat as a checklist, not a script.
  </Step>
  <Step title="6. Adoption Cascade">
    The 12-month projected adoption curve. Useful for forecasting; don't over-fit on the exact slope.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Share the result

Press `⌘E` to export a PDF. The PDF includes:

- Decision question, hypothesis, success criteria
- Overview distribution + confidence + NPS
- Top 5 segments and their breakdowns
- Top 5 objection clusters with sample verbatims
- Sentia recommendations
- Population provenance (which integrations, sample sizes, fidelity score)

This is the artifact to bring to your decision meeting. Annotate it with team disagreements before the meeting.

## What to do with the answer

Three honest scenarios:

| Result | Action |
| --- | --- |
| Sentia confirms what you already believed | Ship faster. Use the simulation as one signal in the decision write-up. |
| Sentia contradicts the team consensus | This is the high-value case. Read the Segments and Objections tabs carefully. Decide whether to: re-scope, A/B test cautiously, or trust the model and ship narrowly. |
| Sentia is split / low confidence | The decision is genuinely contested. Use it as input to a smaller A/B test rather than a full launch. |

## After you ship

Once the feature rolls out (LaunchDarkly > 50%, GitHub merge, or `/shipped` in Slack):

- Sentia auto-detects the ship and opens a **14-day measurement window**
- It pulls actuals from your analytics + support providers
- Predicted vs. actual lands in the Calibration tab of the original simulation
- Per-segment correction factors update — your next simulation in this area is more accurate

See [Calibration](/calibration/overview) for the full loop.
