The single highest-leverage thing you can do early is build a population grounded in your data. This page walks you through the first real one.

## Before you start

Have these connected:

- ✅ **One analytics provider** (Amplitude, Mixpanel, or PostHog)
- ✅ **At least one qualitative source** (Dovetail, Intercom, or Zendesk) — strongly recommended

Without qualitative data, your population will use demographic priors only and top out around 70% normalized accuracy. With interviews, you'll hit ~85%.

## Pick your scope

Start narrow. The instinct to model "everyone" produces blurry populations that converge slowly and read flat. Some good first-population scopes:

- **"All paying customers in our top 3 segments"**
- **"SMB free-trial users in their first 14 days"**
- **"Enterprise admins on the Pro plan"**
- **"Users who triggered the activation event in the last 90 days"**

Each of these is sharp enough to make meaningful predictions and broad enough to be useful across many simulations.

## Build it

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Populations → New">
    Press `P`, then click **+ New population**. The builder opens.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 1: Define">
    Name it ("SMB trial users — May 2026"), pick agent count (start at **1,000** — enough for fast convergence and segment breakdowns), and set the region (US or EU).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 2: Filter">
    Combine **census filters** (age range, income, region, household, education) with **analytics cohorts** (a saved cohort from your provider). Examples:
    
    - Census: 25–54, US, household income $50k–$200k
    - Amplitude cohort: "Trial started, < 14 days, did not complete activation"

    The right-side preview updates live, showing a sample-size projection and a demographic chart.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 3: Ground">
    Pick which qualitative sources to weight into agent memory:
    
    - **Dovetail projects** — interview transcripts, highlights
    - **Intercom conversations** — last 90 days, satisfaction-rated
    - **Zendesk tickets** — last 90 days, resolved
    
    Sentia clusters verbatims into pain points and assigns them to agents by behavioral similarity. You can preview a sample of verbatims that will ground a sample agent.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Step 4: Build">
    Click **Build population**. Generation runs in the background — typically 3–8 minutes for 1,000 agents. You'll see a Slack notification (and an in-app toast) when it's done.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Validate fidelity

Once built, every population should pass the **5-experiment behavioral validation suite** before you trust it for real decisions.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the population">
    Click the population card. The first tab is **Validation**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run validation">
    Click **Run validation**. It executes a sample of agents through 5 canonical behavioral economics experiments (dictator game, trust game, public goods, prisoner's dilemma, ultimatum). ~2 minutes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the score">
    - **Pass rate ≥ 80%** + **Fidelity ≥ 0.7** → healthy. Use the population for simulations.
    - Below either threshold → your population is too generic or too narrow. Add more qualitative data, broaden the cohort, or rebuild with different filters.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Fidelity below 0.7 isn't a "failed" population — it's a signal you need more grounding. The fastest fix is connecting Dovetail interviews. The slowest is broadening census filters.
</Tip>

## A common first-week pattern

Many new workspaces build **two populations** in week one:

1. A broad "Our customers" population (3,000 agents) for high-level roadmap questions
2. A narrow "Active enterprise admins" or "First-week trial users" population (1,000 agents) for focused product changes

You can have many populations. They're cheap to keep around. Simulations bind to one population each.

## Maintenance

Populations slowly go stale as your user base evolves. Sentia automatically:

- Re-syncs your analytics cohorts daily
- Re-embeds new interview transcripts hourly
- Flags populations whose demographic mix has drifted > 10% from baseline (you'll see an amber "Drift detected" badge)

When drift is flagged, rebuild the population — it takes < 10 minutes and keeps accuracy up.
