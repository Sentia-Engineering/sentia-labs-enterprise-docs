Reports are durable research artifacts created from Sentia results. Save them when a simulation influences a design, roadmap, or stakeholder decision.

## What can be saved

| Source | What the saved report contains |
| --- | --- |
| **Simulation result** | Verdict, visuals, metrics, findings, concerns, recommendations, population lens, voices, and methodology. |
| **Simulation comparison** | Side-by-side differences across 2-3 completed runs, including preference shift, confidence, concern density, visual evidence, and trait deltas. |

Saved reports appear in **Resource Library -> Reports**.

## Visibility

| Visibility | Use when |
| --- | --- |
| **Private** | Draft analysis, exploratory work, or evidence you are not ready to share. |
| **Workspace** | Decision reviews, research readouts, design critique, or shared planning. |

Admins can review workspace report scopes and archived reports from settings.

## When to save a report

Save when:

- The run answers a real design or product question.
- You plan to cite the evidence in critique, planning, or a PRD.
- You compared variants and need a stable artifact.
- The report should survive beyond the simulation list filters.

Do not save every exploratory run. Keep the Reports tab focused on evidence your team may return to.

## Recommended naming

Use a decision-oriented title:

```text
Figma onboarding variants - first-time admins - May 2026
Pricing page trust test - enterprise buyers - V2 comparison
Mobile setup flow - accessibility audit follow-up
```
