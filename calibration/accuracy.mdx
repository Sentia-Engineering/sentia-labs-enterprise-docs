The calibration dashboard at `/calibration` is where you read your workspace's overall simulation accuracy. Three views matter most.

## Normalized accuracy (the headline)

A 0–1 score that adjusts raw accuracy for natural human behavioral inconsistency (~81% test-retest baseline).

**Normalized accuracy = 1.0** means Sentia is as accurate at predicting your users as your users are at predicting their own behavior.

| Score | Reading |
| --- | --- |
| > 0.85 | Excellent. Trust predictions for high-stakes decisions. |
| 0.75–0.85 | Good. Use predictions as primary input but stay open to surprises. |
| 0.65–0.75 | Directional only. Calibrate aggressively; ground populations further. |
| < 0.65 | Substantial gap. Audit qualitative grounding and ship-detection coverage. |

### Why "normalized"?

Raw accuracy compares predicted vs. actual one-to-one. But humans themselves only predict their own behavior with ~81% test-retest reliability — meaning a perfect model can only score ~0.81 on raw accuracy.

Normalized accuracy divides by the human ceiling. A score of 1.0 means "as accurate as humans predicting humans." Most workspaces converge to 0.85–0.92 within 30 calibration events.

## The accuracy line chart

The main chart on the dashboard:

- **X axis** — time, default last 90 days
- **Y axis** — normalized accuracy (0–1)
- **Solid line** — workspace overall accuracy
- **Dotted line** — accuracy band (P25–P75 across recent simulations)

**What to look for:**

- **Trending up** → calibration is doing its job
- **Flat at a low number** → consider adding qualitative grounding or experimentation
- **Trending down** → either user-base drift (see [Drift](/calibration/drift)), or stale populations not getting rebuilt

## Segment heatmap

The heatmap shows per-segment accuracy. Rows are segments (adopter category × plan tier × tenure); columns are time buckets.

- **Dark green** — high accuracy in that segment for that period
- **Light** — moderate
- **Red** — poor accuracy

**Common patterns:**

| Pattern | Reading |
| --- | --- |
| Even across rows | Population is well-grounded across all segments |
| One row consistently red | That segment is under-represented in your qualitative grounding |
| Recent columns drifting red | Population needs refreshing; user base evolved |
| Innovator/Early Adopter rows accurate, Late Majority/Laggard rows poor | You may not have many Laggard verbatims in your corpus — they don't reach out as often |

## Correction factors table

The bottom of the dashboard lists active per-segment correction factors:

```
Segment                      Factor    Confidence    Events
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Innovators (Pro)              1.08×    HIGH          24
Early Adopters (Pro)          0.97×    HIGH          22
Early Majority (Free)         0.86×    MEDIUM        12
Late Majority (Free)          0.78×    MEDIUM        8
Laggards (Free)               0.50×    LOW           3
```

| Factor | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **> 1.0** | Sentia has historically *under-predicted* this segment's adoption; future predictions get boosted |
| **= 1.0** | Predictions are accurate; no correction |
| **< 1.0** | Sentia has historically *over-predicted*; future predictions get dampened |

| Confidence | Strength of correction applied |
| --- | --- |
| HIGH (20+ events) | Full correction applied |
| MEDIUM (5–19 events) | 50% applied |
| LOW (< 5 events) | Not applied; baseline used |

## Time-window filters

The dashboard supports:

- **Last 7 / 30 / 90 days** — quick filters
- **Custom range** — pick start/end
- **Per-decision-class** — filter by tag (pricing, onboarding, etc.) to see accuracy within domains
- **Per-population** — filter by which population the calibrated simulations used

Per-domain views are especially useful: "Our pricing predictions are at 0.91, but onboarding is at 0.74" tells you where to focus grounding investment.

## Exporting

Calibration data exports:

- **CSV** — full event list for analysis
- **PDF** — formatted accuracy report for board reviews
- **JSON** — via API at `GET /admin/calibration` (see [API reference](/api-reference/introduction))

## Building stakeholder trust

A common question from execs: *"How do we know we can trust these predictions?"*

The honest answer involves three artifacts:

1. **Normalized accuracy trend** — show the up-and-to-the-right
2. **Per-segment correction factors** — show that Sentia *knows where it's been wrong* and adjusts
3. **A few representative case studies** — pick 3–5 calibrated decisions and walk through predicted vs. actual

This combination wins skeptical executives faster than any single number.
